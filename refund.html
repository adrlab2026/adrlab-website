Refund Policy

Please read this policy carefully before subscribing to ADR Scholar Pro.

Last updated: March 2025

⚠️ No Refunds After Use

ADR Scholar Pro is a digital service. Once you have used any Pro feature — generated ideas, downloaded a template, or created research content — no refund will be issued. Refunds are available only in cases of verified technical failure where our service was unable to generate content.

Why this policy exists: ADR Scholar delivers instant AI-generated value. Once a project report, research paper, or template has been generated and downloaded, that value cannot be returned — similar to how a book purchase or a meal cannot be refunded after use. We have set our pricing as low as possible precisely to make this a fair exchange.

When You ARE Eligible for a Refund

✓ Refund will be issued if:

  • You were charged but our platform was completely down and you could not access any feature
  • A technical error on our side prevented content from being generated after multiple attempts
  • You were charged twice for the same subscription due to a payment processing error
  • You subscribed but never logged in and never used any feature (within 24 hours of payment)

When You Are NOT Eligible for a Refund

✗ No refund will be issued if:

  • You generated more than 3 project ideas using the Idea Generator
  • You downloaded any template — blank or AI-filled — (SRS, Synopsis, Report, IEEE paper)
  • You used any Research Paper Assistant feature — Abstract, Literature Review, Research Gap, Methodology, Results, or Full Paper
  • You generated a Full Research Paper (5 Claude calls)
  • You simply changed your mind or no longer need the service
  • You found the AI output quality unsatisfactory after using it
  • Your institution rejected the AI-generated content
  • More than 24 hours have passed since payment (for unused accounts)
  • You violated our Terms of Service

We strongly recommend using the Free plan before subscribing to Pro. The Free plan lets you generate 3 ideas, download 1 blank template, and use 2 research tool features — fully representative of the Pro experience — so you can evaluate quality before paying.

Consumer Rights

Indian Users — Consumer Protection Act 2019

Under the Consumer Protection Act 2019 and the IT Act 2000, digital service providers are permitted to have no-refund policies for digital goods once delivered and accessed. ADR Scholar's AI-generated content constitutes a delivered digital service. However, if our service was genuinely defective or not as described, your statutory rights under Indian consumer law are not affected by this policy.

EU / EEA Users — Consumer Rights Directive

Under EU Directive 2011/83/EU, the right of withdrawal for digital content begins to lapse once performance has begun with the consumer's prior express consent and acknowledgement. By clicking "Generate" for the first time, you expressly consent to immediate performance and acknowledge that the right of withdrawal will be lost upon generation of digital content. This is consistent with Article 16(m) of the Directive.
