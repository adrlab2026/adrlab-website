Frequently Asked Questions

General
What is ADR Scholar?

ADR Scholar is an AI-powered academic tool designed for BTech and MTech engineering students worldwide. It helps you generate final year project ideas, create SRS documents, project synopsis, full project reports, and IEEE/Springer/APA research papers — all in minutes.

It is a product by ADR Lab, an academic technology company building tools for students, institutions, and companies.

Who is ADR Scholar for?

ADR Scholar is designed primarily for:

  • BTech students in their 5th-8th semester working on final year projects
  • MTech students writing research papers or thesis documents
  • Engineering students who need academic documentation support

While we are built with Indian engineering students in mind, ADR Scholar is available worldwide and works for any student needing academic project support.

AI Content & Academic Integrity
Will my college detect that the content is AI-generated?

ADR Scholar generates content as a starting draft — similar to how a tutor or guide would give you notes or a framework to work from. We strongly recommend that every student:

  • Review all AI-generated content carefully
  • Add their own analysis, actual results, and screenshots
  • Rewrite sections in their own voice
  • Add their specific data, graphs, and experimental findings

The AI gives you the structure and academic language — you provide the actual project content, results, and personal insights. Used this way, the output is genuinely your work supported by AI tools, similar to using Grammarly or Google Scholar.

Is using AI tools for academic work ethical?

This is an evolving area in academia. Most institutions globally are developing policies that permit AI as a writing aid while requiring students to produce original analysis and results. ADR Scholar is designed as a writing assistance tool — not a plagiarism tool.

We always recommend students to check their institution's specific policy on AI-assisted writing before submitting AI-aided work.

Does ADR Lab store or use my documents to train AI?

No. We do not use any content you generate or upload to train AI models. Your project ideas, templates, and research papers belong to you. We only store the minimum metadata required to deliver the service (usage counts, account details). Please refer to our Privacy Policy for full details.

Technical
What file formats does ADR Scholar support?
  • Templates — Downloaded as Microsoft Word (.docx) files, compatible with MS Word, Google Docs, LibreOffice
  • Research Paper Assistant — Generated content displayed on screen with copy and .docx download option
Why is generation taking longer than usual?

ADR Scholar uses Claude AI for content generation. Generation times vary by feature:

  • Project ideas — 10-20 seconds
  • Single research sections (Abstract, Methodology etc.) — 10-20 seconds
  • AI-filled templates (SRS, Synopsis) — 30-60 seconds
  • Full project report (6 chapters) — 60-120 seconds
  • Full research paper — 75-90 seconds

A progress bar shows you exactly which section is being written. If generation fails, please try again — occasional API timeouts can occur during peak usage.

I am getting a 500 error. What should I do?

  • Log out and log back in
  • Check your internet connection
  • Wait 2-3 minutes and try again (occasional API rate limiting)
